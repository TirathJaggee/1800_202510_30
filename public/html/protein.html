Proteins

Proteins are micronutrients required by the body to carry out a range of normal functions...

Proteins are organic substances that are generally classified as either fat soluble or water soluble...

Minerals are inorganic elements present in soil and water...

In the U.S., the National Academy of Medicine develops nutrient reference values called Dietary Reference Intakes (DRIs)...

What Is Protein?

Protein is found throughout the body—in muscle, bone, skin, hair...

Protein is made from twenty-plus basic building blocks called amino acids...

How Much Protein Do I Need?

The National Academy of Medicine recommends that adults get a minimum of 0.8 grams of protein per kg of body weight.

  • 100g chicken breast: 31g protein
  • 100g beef: 26g protein
  • 100g tofu: 19g protein
  • 100g eggs: 13g protein
  • 100g fish: 18g protein
  • 140 lbs: ~50g/day
  • 180 lbs: ~65g/day
  • 220 lbs: ~80g/day
  • 260 lbs: ~95g/day
  • 300 lbs: ~110g/day

The National Academy of Medicine allows 10–35% of calories from protein...

Protein deficiency can lead to serious health issues...

Most adults in developed countries consume more than enough protein.
