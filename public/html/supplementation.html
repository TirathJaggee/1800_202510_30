Supplementation

Supplementation is the process of adding nutrients to the diet to ensure your body receives what it needs to function properly.

The Dietary Supplement Label

Supplements come with a Supplement Facts label showing active ingredients, dosage, and other substances like fillers or binders. While manufacturers suggest serving sizes, your healthcare provider may recommend a different amount.

Effectiveness

Supplements may help meet nutrient needs when your diet lacks variety. But they cannot replace a healthy eating routine.

Some supplements may help manage health conditions, such as:

  • Calcium & Vitamin D for bone strength
  • Omega-3 for heart health & inflammation
  • Vitamin D for bone fracture prevention
  • Folic acid for reducing birth defect risk

However, many supplements still need further research, and the FDA does not confirm their effectiveness before marketing.

Fortified foods may also lead to excess intake. For example, too much Vitamin A can cause liver damage or birth defects, and excess iron may harm organs.

Pregnant or nursing? Only take supplements recommended by a healthcare provider. The same caution applies to children.

If you experience a reaction to a supplement, notify your doctor. You can also report it to the FDA via 800-FDA-1088 or their website.
